### **Bio**

Floe Foxon is an interdisciplinary scientist who specialises in modelling and simulation. He holds a Master of Science degree in Data Science (Statistics) from the University of Leeds with a Distinction award, and a First Class Honours degree in Physics with Astronomy from the University of Nottingham. As an undergraduate student, Floe was awarded a First Year Scholarship Award for Academic Achievement; completed a research scholarship with Sanford Research on population health modelling; and was published in peer-reviewed journals twice for his university project on stellar simulation modelling in *Astronomy and Astronomy*, and his personal project on cryptography in the *Journal of Young Investigators*. Since then, Floe has published dozens of works on varied topics from astronomy and public health to cryptography and zoology, using a wide range of statistical and computational methods including machine learning, system dynamics, Bayesian statistics, and generalized linear models for prediction and for inference. His first book, *Folklore and Zoology*, was published by *CRC Press* in 2024. Floe has presented his works internationally at scientific conferences including *HistoCrypt*, the meeting of the *American Astronomical Society*, and the *Society for Research on Nicotine and Tobacco* annual meetings. Floe's works have featured in *Science*, *New Scientist*, *Popular Mechanics* and others, earning widespread recognition for his 'debunking' of pseudoscientific claims with statistical analysis.
